The Problem

Exchange is life.
The conditions are not.

The most important exchanges — a payment, a health record, a work assignment, an agreement — happen in the places where infrastructure is least reliable. Dead zones. Failing batteries. No signal. No cloud. The world's most critical communications collapse at exactly the moment they matter most.

ZAKO is built for those conditions as its baseline. Not as a fallback. Not as a degraded mode. As the design premise.

  • No internet. No cloud. Records are written, signed, and chain-hashed locally. No resolver needed. No upload required.
  • Battery at 8%. Outstack governs every process. Critical exchange stays alive. Non-essential work suspends.
  • SMS only. ZAKO treats SMS, BLE, QR, and IP as roads — not the network. The network is the protocol.
  • Counterparty offline. Peer-to-peer exchange with a shared truth anchor. Sync when available. Verifiable either way.
  • Credentials needed now. Every credential is verifiable against the Sovereign's public key alone. No network call. No authority lookup.
The Exchange Protocol

Telux

Tel — to communicate. Exchange is the essence of communication.

Telux is the full-stack exchange system. Identity exists to enable exchange. The ledger exists to make exchange verifiable. Power governance exists because the most critical exchange must survive the harshest conditions.

At every scale — daemon to daemon, peer to peer, device to distribution — the same protocol governs. One record format. One signing model. One truth mechanism. Fractal coherence from the smallest IPC call to cross-network exchange.

Telux does not depend on any carrier. It does not require any network to be "the network." It runs on top of all of them.

Power Governance

Outstack

Telux was built to sustain the most critical exchange under the harshest conditions. That goal demanded its own answer: a power governor that treats energy as a ledger and process class as law. That answer is Outstack.

When battery drops, Outstack does not randomly throttle. It applies a precise, principled execution matrix. Every process on the device is declared at one of five classes. Every power mode gates a defined tier. Critical exchange survives what lesser systems cannot.

Immutable Truth

The Ledger

Every meaningful event on a ZAKO device — a payment, a task completed, a health record, a credential earned, a message sent — produces a signed, chain-hashed record written to an append-only ledger.

Records are never modified. Never deleted. The chain is the proof. A record's validity is verifiable locally, offline, without any external call, using only the Sovereign's public key and the chain hash of the record before it.

Data Sovereignty

Islands

A Sovereign's data is not a database row owned by a provider. It lives in sovereign containers — Islands — each holding a distinct class of life records: exchange, identity, health, work, learning.

Each Island is a named, access-controlled, cryptographically chained partition of the Sovereign's ledger. The Sovereign controls exactly what is shared with whom, under what conditions, for how long. No provider can read what the Sovereign has not disclosed.

Health records default to RESTRICT_FORWARD. Journal entries are sealed by SELinux policy. Work Islands are scoped to employer relationships. Every boundary is enforced at protocol level — not at the application level.

Selective disclosure. Per-record. Per-counterparty. Without any platform intermediary. The Sovereign holds the keys — literally.

Integration Standard

SIMBA

Service Integration for Mobile Business Applications

Inside ZAKO, there is one process model, one record format, one power governor, one design language. A service seeking ZAKO integration adopts all of them.

SIMBA defines the terms. Every admitted service becomes a node on the ZAKO device network — with a declared identity, declared process class, declared record model, and declared network behaviour. Not a guest. A participant.

Offline-First by Design

Any network.
No network.

ZAKO does not live inside a carrier network. It lives above all of them. SMS, Bluetooth, QR, IP — these are roads. ZAKO is the vehicle. The vehicle does not depend on the road to know where it is.

Records are written, signed, and chained on-device before any transmission occurs. When connectivity is available, records synchronise. When it isn't, nothing breaks. The ledger doesn't wait for the cloud.

A payment sent over SMS carries the same verifiable signature as one sent over IP. A credential shared by QR code is as valid as one delivered over a secure API. The carrier does not confer validity. The signature does.
