A Cat S22 Flip, locked behind a PIN no one remembers.

Ruggedized, palm-sized, built for job sites. Useless until you wipe it.

The stock Android recovery menu is two button presses deep. Factory reset. Thirty seconds. The phone reboots to a blank setup screen — no accounts, no data, no assumptions. Just a small slab of computing potential asking a question it has never been allowed to ask before:

What do you put on it?

Not what Google suggests. Not what Bullitt shipped. What do you put on a phone when you're starting from nothing and answering to no one?

That question is the origin of ZAKO.

01Every Phone Serves Its Manufacturer

Here's the thing about your phone — every phone, regardless of what it cost or who sold it to you. It maintains relationships you didn't choose.

Android, as it reaches you from any manufacturer, is built around continuous check-in with Google's infrastructure. Push notifications route through Google's servers. Application signatures verify against Google's keys. Location history, usage patterns, crash reports — all of it flows to data centers in jurisdictions the phone's user has never visited.

Technical · GMS Power Draw

Google Mobile Services maintains persistent connections that draw 60–120mW continuously. On a 1450mAh (5.4Wh) battery, that's 1–2% of total capacity per hour consumed purely on maintaining Google's infrastructure relationships. Verified by packet capture on stock firmware.

Apple's approach is structurally similar and in many ways more complete. The phone is a terminal into Apple's services. Its value proposition depends on that integration.

Neither is dishonest about what it is. Both are internally coherent. Both serve hundreds of millions of people well enough.

But there are other purposes.

02The Reversed Premise

There are contexts — growing in number — where the phone must serve its user rather than its manufacturer. Where the device must be sovereign before it is connected. Where the records belong to the parties involved and to no one else.

A phone in Lusaka managing mobile money. An autonomous excavator in Mongolia. A hospital assistant in Lagos. A relay node on a spacecraft between Earth and Mars.

Context · Deployment Scenarios

ZAKO supports four deployment profiles from the Standard level: personal mobile (Zambia first), autonomous industrial (offline-first, power-governed), medical/institutional (health records with RESTRICT_FORWARD), and deep-space relay (light-speed latency, no Earth dependency). The Standard does not change. The distribution profile changes.

ZAKO is built for those contexts. It doesn't argue the existing ecosystem is wrong. It argues the world contains purposes that require something it was not designed to provide.

03Writing Was Invented for Ledgers

The first writing systems were not literature. They were ledgers. Cuneiform tablets from Uruk, 3100 BCE. Grain allocations. Debt obligations. Writing was invented so exchange could be trusted across time and distance.

Context · Cuneiform Origins

The Uruk IV/III period (c. 3200–2900 BCE) produced the earliest known writing — overwhelmingly administrative: goods, personnel, land. The clay token system preceding cuneiform dates to ~8000 BCE. Writing emerged when token complexity exceeded physical management.

The clay token preceded the word. The record preceded the story.

ZAKO is an attempt to build the next such substrate. An operating system whose primary purpose is to make exchange trustworthy, sovereign, and legible. Not a ledger application on a generic platform. A platform where the ledger is infrastructure.

That's the premise. Everything that follows is engineering.

04Lineage — What ZAKO Inherits

From Unix: The Composable Small Tool

Systems composed of small, independently auditable, loosely coupled components are more robust than monolithic systems. ZAKO's power governor is a daemon. Its exchange layer is three separate processes. They can fail in isolation, and the system continues in a degraded but defined state.

From Plan 9: The Namespace as Primitive

Every process has its own private namespace. Plan 9's ideas propagated through Linux namespaces into Docker and Kubernetes. ZAKO's Island model is Plan 9's namespace doctrine applied to group exchange.

Technical · Plan 9 → Containers

Plan 9 per-process namespaces (1992) → Linux CLONE_NEWNS (2002) → Linux namespaces (2006–2013) → Docker (2013) → Kubernetes (2014). ZAKO's Island model applies the same isolation insight to group exchange rather than process isolation.

From seL4: The Capability as Unforgeable Token

Formally verified. Capability tokens: unforgeable, delegatable, revocable. No ambient authority. ZAKO inherits sovereignty as capability — ed25519 keys backed by hardware.

Technical · Capability Model

The Sovereign's ed25519 key (TrustZone-backed) is the root capability. Island membership is a signed GRANT record (task_code=0x08). Delegation limited to 3 levels. Revocation (0x09) cascades immediately. No grace period. No ambient authority.

From CICS: The Transaction as Substrate

IBM transaction monitors, continuous since the 1960s. Every operation commits or rolls back. Recorded before it executes. Durable. Reconstructible. ZAKO's exchange ledger aspires to this: not an application concern but an infrastructure property.

05The Five Foundational Principles

Every Interaction Is a Record

Something happened. State changed. Two sides exist. Both are recorded. Amendable but never deletable.

Conservation at the Wire Level

A batch that doesn't balance cannot be formed. Structural, not procedural. Five bytes for double-entry.

Technical · BitLedger 40-bit Format

Five bytes encode a complete double-entry transaction. Conservation is structural — the encoding makes imbalanced records impossible to construct. Three independent error detection: CRC-15, cross-layer mirroring, conservation invariant. Same format for money, energy, mass, data, time, service obligations.

Sovereignty Is the Design Constraint

Records don't live on external servers. Identity doesn't come from third parties. Data doesn't leave without explicit sovereign action. ed25519 + TrustZone + W3C DIDs + capability grants.

Offline-First Is Non-Negotiable

Four channels: SMS, QR code, Bluetooth LE, IP. A device that requires the network has outsourced its sovereignty.

Power Is a Sovereignty Property

A device that cannot control power consumption cannot guarantee availability. Power events are records. The power budget is a ledger. Spacecraft discipline applied to phones.

06The Three-Layer Architecture

Bedrock

Hardware and kernel. TrustZone, dm-verity, Telux-SEC LSM, hardware power gating. These aren't policies. They're physics.

Submerged

Outstack (power governor), Telux daemon triad (ledgerd, identd, sharedb), identity service. Reads from Bedrock, expresses policy upward.

Visible

Applications, telephony, natural language query. Built on AOSP. No Google Mobile Services. The surface serves its Sovereign.

07The Roadmap Ahead

The Babb Cat is the proving ground. Not a product yet. A laboratory. The experiments: boot on Zambian carriers, Outstack on real hardware, Telux on 2GB RAM, mobile money on live SIMs, the cover display as sovereignty surface.

Each experiment produces records. Each record feeds back. The architecture adapts. The sovereignty doesn't.

Part IIThe Engineering

08BitPads: One Byte Tells You Everything

Every ZAKO transmission starts with a single Meta byte. Before the receiver processes a single bit of payload, that byte declares what's coming.

Technical · Frame Type Encoding

Meta byte upper 2 bits: 00=Pure Signal, 01=Anonymous Wave, 10=Full Record, 11=Full BitLedger. Lower 6 bits carry priority, domain hints, continuation flags. Self-describing from byte one.

SizeFrameUse
1 bytePure SignalHeartbeat, daemon ACK, power alert
4 bytesAnonymous WaveSensor readings, pictography
13–29 bytesFull RecordWork records, journal, health
22–44+ bytesFull BitLedgerPayments, exchanges, grants

09BitLedger: Five Bytes for Double-Entry

40-bit double-entry. Five bytes for any conserved scalar exchange. Conservation enforced at encoding time — structurally impossible to form an imbalanced batch.

Technical · Conservation Enforcement

Three independent mechanisms: CRC-15, cross-layer direction/status mirroring, conservation invariant. Universal Domain extension: same 40-bit format for money, energy, mass, data, time, service. Domain declaration in session header changes semantics, not wire format.

The same format for money, energy, mass, data volume, time, service obligations. The wire format is identical. Only the domain declaration changes what the bits mean.

1064 Verbs, 4096 Meanings

task_code (6 bits) × domain (2 bits) × account_pair (4 bits) = 4,096 distinct record semantics. One format for payments, work assignments, health readings, capability grants, power transitions, contract amendments, spacecraft telemetry.

And one bit for propagation: RESTRICT_FORWARD. Set it, the record does not leave the device. Sovereignty at the field level.

11Outstack — Power as Organizing Principle

Not power management. The organizing principle of the entire runtime. Spacecraft discipline: every subsystem has a budget, graceful degradation by design.

The Five Modes

ModeBatteryBehavior
FULL>80%No constraints
STANDARD40–80%OPPORTUNISTIC gated
CONSERVATION20–40%BACKGROUND paused
CRITICAL5–20%Only CRITICAL + INTERACTIVE
EMERGENCY<5%Survival posture

Hysteretic transitions. 3% band. No oscillation.

Technical · Babb Cat Implementation

outstack-powerd: 60s battery poll, netlink uevents, cgroup hierarchy per class (CPU shares, memory limits, freezer). xattr-based class assignment, SELinux-protected. Lid sensor: flip closed → CONSERVE. eDRX: disabled → 5.12s → 10.24s → 20.48s → 40.96s. Target: 8mA idle = 72–108h standby from 1450mAh.

Execution Gating

Every exec() evaluated by a Linux Security Module. Kernel boundary, not policy preference. A compromised process cannot escalate its class.

Power Anomaly as Security

Hardware power gating: Outstack removes power from peripheral domains. A WiFi chip without power cannot transmit. Physics, not policy.

12Telux — The Exchange Layer

A single device is a network. Same protocol at every scale — internal daemon communication uses the same wire format as cross-continent SMS payments.

The Daemon Triad

telux-ledgerd (CRITICAL) — append-only, fsync before ACK, always.
telux-identd (CRITICAL) — key custodian, refuses signing when locked.
telux-sharedb (INTERACTIVE) — outbound channel selection.

Technical · Ledger Integrity

fsync() on SQLite WAL before ACK emission. No deferred write option. Chain hash: BLAKE3(frame_hash[n] || chain_hash[n-1]). Genesis: BLAKE3(frame_hash[0] || zeros_32). Tamper with one record and every subsequent hash breaks.

Islands and Sovereignty

Six axioms. Constraints enforced at three tiers: VISIBLE (policy), SUBMERGED (cgroups), BEDROCK (kernel LSM). A BEDROCK constraint cannot be circumvented by software.

Identity Chain

ed25519 in TrustZone. W3C DIDs (did:key:z6Mk...). BLAKE3 chain hashes. Capability grants depth-limited to 3. Revocation cascades immediately.

13The Four Services

PADS — Field Records

Up to 16 work Islands. INTERACTIVE for activity, BACKGROUND for sync. In Conservation, you can still fill out a form.

Health — Conserved Quantities

Blood pressure, glucose, weight, temperature. RESTRICT_FORWARD=1 on reproductive/mental health. Alerts always INTERACTIVE. Never deferred.

Academy — Learning as Ledger

XP from records, not mutable balances. Content via BLAKE3-addressed nodes. Streak miss = break.

Agreements — Contracts as Records

PROPOSE → ACCEPT → ACTIVE → COMPLETE → EXPIRE. Hosted Ledger Nodes for multi-party. CREATE → JOIN → AGREE.

Part IIIThe Context

15De-Googling — The Founding Decision

Zero GMS packages. Zero GMS traffic — verified by packet capture. 60–120mW eliminated. All Google data transmission eliminated. The founding decision: remove the infrastructure, replace with Telux.

Technical · GMS Replacements

Push: FCM → UnifiedPush + ntfy. Apps: Play Store → F-Droid (privileged, auditable). Location: Google FLP → AOSP GPS + Mozilla. Auth: Google Account → Telux DID. Maps: Organic Maps (offline, no telemetry). Attestation: HSM direct (narrower but honest).

Every replacement follows one rule: more sovereign, not merely different.

16Zambia — The Design Center

Not a test market. The design center. Mobile money is the primary financial infrastructure. Electricity irregular. Data expensive. No existing Android OS built for this context.

Context · Carriers & Mobile Money

Airtel (Bands 3/7/8, *778#, VoLTE), Zamtel (Bands 7/40, *303#), MTN (Band 3, *112#). All within QM215 band set. STK kept alive — CRITICAL priority. USSD tested on live SIMs before every release. If mobile money doesn't work, the build doesn't ship.

Mobile Money as Primary Use Case

STK and USSD at CRITICAL priority. A user with 5% battery can still check balance and send money. That's the requirement.

Language and Input

en_ZM, Africa/Lusaka. T9 with Bemba, Nyanja, Tonga, Lozi. First-run: no external account creation. The device is yours from the first screen.
